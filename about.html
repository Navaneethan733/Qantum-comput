/ Mission /

Vertical system integration is the only path.

Superconducting qubits are highly sensitive to external fields, microwave calibration drifts, and compilation overheads. We believe scaling can only be achieved by building the dilution refrigerators, custom microwave control chips, error compilers, and remote cloud layers under one roof.

  • Superconducting chip fabrication in-house
  • Custom control electronics built for low temperature
  • Open API integrations with standard SDK libraries

/ Company Timeline /

Our trajectory.

2022

16-Qubit Cryostat Prototype

Demonstrated our first closed-loop dilution refrigerator operating at 15mK with microwave control lines mapping 16 superconducting qubits. Achieved 98.4% single-gate fidelity.

2023

Cloud Access and SDK Integrations

Opened REST APIs to active academic researchers and integrated our compiler routing directly into Qiskit. Scaled chip size to 64 physical qubits.

2025

General Availability of 128-Qubit Platform

Released the General Availability tier of our 128-qubit hardware platform with 24/7 telemetry monitoring and pulse-level microwave microwave controls.

2027 (Vision)

Fault-Tolerant Logical Qubits

Implement surface code error correction across logical clusters on a 1 000-qubit processor framework to deliver scale advantage for materials physics.

/ Corporate Philosophy /

Our values.

01. ⚖

Co-Design Philosophy

We do not optimize hardware in isolation. Our compiler developers sit adjacent to our cryo-electronics engineers to align layout rules and compile metrics.

02. 🔓

Scientific Openness

We actively publish calibrations, error statistics, and hardware models. We support open source libraries and prioritize academic collaborations.

03. 🔬

Extreme Precision

In the quantum domain, microscopic temperature shifts or sub-nano-degree phase offsets destroy computation. We operate with strict system tolerances.
